# About

**[Doikayt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doikayt)** is Yiddish for "hereness." The term
refers to the core concept of the Bundists — members of the General Jewish Labour Bund,
a secular socialist movement active in the Russian Empire and Poland beginning around 1897.

Bundists were explicitly non- or anti-Zionist, holding that Jews should build justice
and a good life wherever they live, in solidarity with other secular, multi-ethnic,
working-class movements — rather than deferring collective liberation to a national
homeland elsewhere.
